# T011 worklog — Perfumes: weight Fragrantica vs LLM research by confidence

## Attempt 1 — 2026-06-21

### Problem
Niche houses get very few Fragrantica community votes (corpus min was 18 votes),
so their longevity/sillage/season/gender/rating distributions are noisy and
shouldn't outweigh the LLM's own multi-source web research. Designer
blockbusters get tens of thousands of votes (corpus max 21,809) — there the
community signal is the most reliable source. The build prompt previously told
Claude to "treat [Fragrantica] as the community-signal source of truth" with no
notion of sample size, so a 18-vote niche fragrance and a 21k-vote designer were
trusted equally.

### Approach (continuous, corpus-calibrated)
- **Continuous confidence weight** `votes/(votes+k)` in `[0,1)`
  (`confidenceWeight`), 0.5 at `votes == k`, 0 when there are no votes (defer
  entirely to research).
- **Corpus calibration:** `voteDistribution` reads the vote count off EVERY
  scraped `data/out/fragrantica/*.json` (`loadVoteCorpus` in `lib.ts`) and sets
  `k` to the corpus MEDIAN vote count by default — so "high" vs "low" is judged
  relative to *this* ecosystem, not an arbitrary absolute. The whole
  distribution (min/p25/median/p75/max) is logged and fed into the prompt.
  Override with `PERFUMES_CONFIDENCE_K`; empty corpus → `DEFAULT_CONFIDENCE_K=50`.
- **Explicit in output:** `confidenceClause` injects the numeric weight, the
  perfume's band vs the corpus, a lean directive (≥0.5 → lean on Fragrantica;
  <0.5 → lean on web research, treat votes as a weak prior), AND requires Claude
  to state "Community-signal confidence: NN% …" in the Community Sentiment
  section of the built profile.

  default null = calibrate to corpus median).

- `src/jobs/perfumes/build.ts`: `VoteDistribution`, `DEFAULT_CONFIDENCE_K`,

  `votesFromFragJson()`, `confidenceClause()`; `runBuild` computes the
  distribution once + logs it; `buildPrompt` takes the distribution and inserts
  the confidence clause before the mapping rules.
- `src/jobs/perfumes/build.test.ts`: corpus distribution/median calibration,
  override + empty fallback, weight monotonicity/0.5-at-k/null→0,
  `votesFromFragJson`, and the LOW (18 votes) vs HIGH (21,809 votes) fixtures
  asserting the relative weighting (wLow<wHigh, low<0.5<high, blend % crosses
  50, low clause leans on research / high leans on Fragrantica, both route the
  confidence into the profile).
- `README.md`: perfumes pipeline description updated.
- `.harness/LIMITATIONS.md`: prompt-enforced (not post-checked) weighting +
  corpus-snapshot calibration trade-offs.
